The history of our rivers

Nestled deep in the Frank Church Wilderness of No Return winds the Salmon river. The Salmon was given the nickname long ago as the River of No Return by the people living near it because of the number of folks that came to visit and fell so in love with the rugged beauty of the river that they never left. Herds of elk and deer graze on the hillsides around the river and bear and bighorn sheep are also commonly sighted.

The Green River winds through remote canyons in central Utah, flowing toward the Colorado River. This 84-mile trip from Sand Wash to Swaseys Rapid features stunning cliffs, desert scenery, and exciting rapids. Named by explorer John Wesley Powell in 1869, Desolation Canyon is a National Landmark known for its natural beauty and rich history. It’s rated Class II/III and usually takes 5 to 6 days to run.

This is the grand daddy of all canyons and river trips. The Grand Canyon offers a breathtaking adventure with world-class whitewater rapids and stunning scenery you will never forget. Along the river, you’ll find thrilling rapids, side canyons to explore, and ancient Native American ruins accessible only by boat, making it a truly unforgettable journey.
